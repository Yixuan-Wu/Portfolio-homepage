PROJECT BACKGROUND

As a Gmail user, I usually check my emails on Gmail app and reply simple emails when my laptop is not with me. But I found that some buttons are hard to click and many options are hidden. I decide to challenge myself to take part of redesigning Gmail application.

PROBLEM

"How might I make it more intuitive and easy to navigate?"

SOLUTION

- Changed "hamburger menu" to bottom navigation
- Simplied unnecessary options

  RESEARCH




Initially, my idea to start this project came from my personal experience when interactiong with Gmail app. But for redesigning the project, my personal experienece is not enough.
My goals for the research was I wanted to talk to people who used Gmail on mobile to get insights of possible changes it could be done. In order to gain more insights, I interviewd 5 of my firends who are student, workers and professionals.

Questions

I introduced my purposes and asked a couple of warm up questions I then asked specific questions such as:

1: Do you use Gmail app version or web version?
2: Usually, in what situation you use Gmail app?
2: What do you like the most and what you don’t like that much on Gmail?
3 :Which other email app have you used?


Key findings

1: They found a bit annoying using the “hamburger menu” to switch options.
2: They mostly use the app to quick scan contents of the emails, rely quick emails, and not often answered emails on mobile specially for important emails.
3: They found that it is unnecessary to show all emails related one user’s accounts on the same inboxes


Decision

After having those insights in mind, I decided to focus on redesign the “hamburger menu”, simplifying the sections and options.

Lightning Demos

I went through some similar products like Yahoo, Mail Master and Gmail web version for inspiration

  REDESIGN




I started my redesign by utilizing the “Thumb Zone” for placing the important buttons

  Reflections

This was a fun and quick personal project, mostly because it is really audacious to perform a redesign of an app like Gmail in less than a week with no deep research or real user data to analysis. But this was meaningfull because:

- I could, in a short period of time make decisions based on my analysis and not on my personal taste.

- I could see the value of research as much insight came from research, almost all of them

- I was able to practice my skills in using various tools, PROTOTYPE(Adobe XD), ILLUSTRATION(Illustrator,Photoshop), ANIMATION(After Effects).
